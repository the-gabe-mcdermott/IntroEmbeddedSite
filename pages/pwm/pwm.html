PWM

Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) is a practice used for a few different applications.

In PWM, your software generates a series of "pulses" on an output pin of varying lengths. One common application is for creating a dimmable LED.

For a brighter LED, longer PWM pulses are used.

To dim the LED, software switches to generating a shorter width pulse.

The pulses rapidly toggle the LED on and off. The shorter pulses toggle the LED on for less time than the longer pulses do, making the LED appear dimmer.
